YSR Rythu Bharosa - PM Kisan Scheme Guidelines (Andhra Pradesh)
==============================================================
1. About the Scheme:
YSR Rythu Bharosa is a welfare scheme implemented by the Government of Andhra Pradesh to provide financial assistance to farmers. The scheme is integrated with PM-Kisan, and it is one of the key welfare initiatives for the farming community in the state of Andhra Pradesh.

2. Benefits:
Eligible farmer families receive a total of Rs. 13,500 per year as investment support.
The assistance is deposited directly into bank accounts in three installments:
- First Installment: Rs. 7,500 in May (during the sowing season, includes Rs. 2,000 from PM-Kisan).
- Second Installment: Rs. 4,000 in October (during the harvesting season/Rabi preparation, includes Rs. 2,000 from PM-Kisan).
- Third Installment: Rs. 2,000 in January (during Sankranti festival, includes Rs. 2,000 from PM-Kisan).

3. Eligibility:
- The scheme is applicable to all landholder farmer families in Andhra Pradesh owning cultivable land.
- Crucially, it also covers Tenant Farmers (cultivators) who belong to Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), Backward Classes (BC), and Minority communities. Tenant farmers receive the same financial assistance of Rs. 13,500 per year from the state government's own budget.
- Landless tenant farmers must obtain a Crop Cultivator Rights Card (CCRC) to be eligible.
- The scheme covers cultivators of food grains, commercial crops, horticulture, and aquaculture.

4. Registration and Verification:
- Village Volunteers and Village Agriculture Assistants (VAAs) at the Rythu Bharosa Kendra (RBK) levels conduct door-to-door verification and register the land details.
- Beneficiary list is published at the RBK office for social audit and transparency.

5. Additional State Subsidies:
- Free borewells under YSR Jala Kala.
- 9 hours of free daytime electricity for agricultural pumpsets.
- Zero-interest loans (Vaddi Leni Runalu) for farmers who repay loans on time.
- Input subsidy for crop loss due to heavy rains or drought.
